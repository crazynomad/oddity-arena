不要温和地走进那个良夜

Do Not Go Gentle into That Good Night
Dylan Thomas · 1951
迪兰·托马斯于1947年写下这首维兰内尔，彼时他的父亲——一位退休的英语教师——正在失明，日渐衰弱。这首诗是英语世界最常被诵读的诗歌之一：对死亡的怒斥，对光明的执念，对至亲的绝望祈求。
Dylan Thomas wrote this villanelle in 1947 as his father — a retired English teacher — was going blind and growing frail. It has become one of the most recited poems in the English language: a defiant cry against mortality, a fierce attachment to light, a desperate prayer to a dying father.
开篇 · The Imperative

不要温和地走进那个良夜，

老年应当在日暮时分燃烧与咆哮；

怒斥，怒斥光明的消逝。

Do not go gentle into that good night,

Old age should burn and rave at close of day;

Rage, rage against the dying of the light.

智者 · The Wise

智慧的人临终虽然懂得黑暗有理，

因为他们的话语未曾迸发出闪电，

也不会温和地走进那个良夜。

Though wise men at their end know dark is right,

Because their words had forked no lightning they

Do not go gentle into that good night.

善者 · The Good

善良的人，当最后的浪潮涌过，哭喊着

他们脆弱的善行本可在绿色的海湾里起舞，

怒斥，怒斥光明的消逝。

Good men, the last wave by, crying how bright

Their frail deeds might have danced in a green bay,

Rage, rage against the dying of the light.

狂者 · The Wild

狂野的人，追逐并歌唱过翱翔的太阳，

才知道，为时太晚，他们令它途中悲伤，

也不要温和地走进那个良夜。

Wild men who caught and sang the sun in flight,

And learn, too late, they grieved it on its way,

Do not go gentle into that good night.

肃者 · The Grave

严肃的人，临近死亡，以炫目的视觉看见

失明的眼可以像流星般闪耀欢欣，

怒斥，怒斥光明的消逝。

Grave men, near death, who see with blinding sight

Blind eyes could blaze like meteors and be gay,

Rage, rage against the dying of the light.

致父亲 · The Father

而您，我的父亲，在那悲伤的高处，

我祈求您，此刻用您炽烈的泪水诅咒我，祝福我。

不要温和地走进那个良夜。

怒斥，怒斥光明的消逝。

And you, my father, there on the sad height,

Curse, bless, me now with your fierce tears, I pray.

Do not go gentle into that good night.

Rage, rage against the dying of the light.

关于维兰内尔
On the Villanelle
维兰内尔是一种严格的诗歌形式，源自文艺复兴时期的法国：全诗十九行，五个三行节加一个四行节，只使用两个韵脚，两句副歌交替出现，最终在末节汇合。迪兰·托马斯将这种形式推向了极致——每一次重复不是简单的回响，而是愈加炽烈的呐喊。副歌像潮水一样反复涌来，每一次都携带着更深的绝望与更猛烈的反抗。
The villanelle is a strict poetic form from Renaissance France: nineteen lines across five tercets and one closing quatrain, built on only two rhymes, with two refrains alternating and converging in the final stanza. Thomas pushed this form to its limit — each recurrence is not a mere echo but an ever more incandescent cry. The refrains return like waves, each time carrying deeper despair and fiercer defiance.
迪兰·托马斯（1914–1953），威尔士诗人，以炽烈的语言和音乐般的韵律著称。此诗写于1947年，1952年发表于诗集《在乡间沉睡》。托马斯于1953年在纽约去世，年仅三十九岁。
Dylan Thomas (1914–1953), Welsh poet renowned for his incandescent language and musical cadence. Written in 1947, this poem was published in 1952 in In Country Sleep. Thomas died in New York in 1953, aged thirty-nine.
